Thank you for participating in this experiment

The Experiment: In this experiment, you will be given several questions where each question contains a picture of many Youtube thumbnails videos. You will be asked to select one of the thumbnail that you think you will likely to click. As a Youtube user, you will be familiar with the setup as the picture replicates the Youtube recommendation system. You always see this page right after finishing watching a Youtube video.

Answering Questions: You can select the thumbnail by selecting the order number of the thumbnail. In selecting the best thumbnail, you should consider the title and the image of each video thumbnail. An answer is always required and only one thumbnail can be chosen. There are no right or wrong answers here beyond your own intuition. You should answer each question as quickly as you can, but without rushing. The survey would take around 5 minutes.

Example Questions: The experiment will be started by 5 example questions. You are expected to answer all the 5 example questions correctly to indicate that you understand the experiment set up completely.

Purpose of the research: This experiment is conducted under the course ‘Experimentation in Psychology and Linguistics’ at the Utrecht University, The Netherlands. The aim of this experiment is to collect data and analyze human behaviour on choosing a Youtube video recommendation. What behaviour exactly cannot be shared to participant to avoid any influence on choosing the answer.

Consent to Participate: By completing this survey, you are giving informed consent to provide data for research and education purposes, as specified in the information sheet below. You are free to withdraw from the experiment. The experimenters will pre-process the data in an anonymous and confidential manner. The data we collect, in anonymous and aggregated format, may be made available for research and teaching purposes. Research results may be published in journal articles, conference presentations and via any other mode of scientific exchange and dissemination that will be seen as appropriate by the researchers, while protecting the participants’ anonymity. Voluntary participation and freedom to withdraw from the researchYour choice to take part in the research is entirely voluntary. You are completely free to choose not to participate, or to withdraw from the study at any moment without any consequence. For any further doubt or clarification request, do not hesitate to ask!
